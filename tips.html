Fishing Tips & Best Practices

  • Check Local Conditions — Always verify weather, water clarity, and recent reports before you go. Early morning and late evening often produce the best bites; adjust your schedule for local sunrise and sunset times.
  • Match Lures to Conditions — Choose lure size and color based on water clarity and species. Bright, high-contrast lures work well in stained water; subtle, natural colors are better in clear water.
  • Practice Safe Handling — When practicing catch-and-release, keep fish wet, minimize air exposure, and use barbless hooks when possible. Carry a basic first-aid kit and wear sun protection and a life jacket on boats.
  • Keep a Fishing Log — Record location, time, bait, water conditions, and results. Over time this helps you identify patterns and improves your success on future trips.
