Species Overview

  • Largemouth Bass — Preferred habitats: weedy shorelines and structure. Largemouth bass are ambush predators that favor cover; try fishing near submerged logs, weed edges, and docks during low-light periods.
  • Channel Catfish — Active at dusk and night; bait anglers often succeed. Channel catfish feed heavily after dark and respond well to stink baits, cut bait, and prepared dough baits.
  • Rainbow Trout — Cold-water streams and stocked lakes; use light tackle. Trout prefer cooler, well-oxygenated water and often take small spinners, flies, or natural baits.
  • Bluegill — Shallow weedy bays; great for beginners and kids. Bluegill are eager biters on small jigs, live worms, and bread, making them ideal for family outings.
